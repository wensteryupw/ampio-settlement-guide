TASK 1 Executive Case Summary
Parties
  • Lead Plaintiffs: Tao Wang and Synworld Technologies Corporation (individually and on behalf of all others similarly situated).
  • Lead Counsel: Faruqi & Faruqi, LLP (James M. Wilson, Jr.).
  • Defendants: Ampio Pharmaceuticals, Inc.; Michael A. Martino; Holli Cherevka; Dan Stokely; David Bar-Or; Philip H. Coelho; Richard B. Giles. (Note: Michael Macaluso was named in the original complaint but has since passed away and is not a party to the settlement.)
  • Defendants' Counsel: Ballard Spahr LLP; Brownstein Hyatt Farber Schreck LLP; Pillsbury Winthrop Shaw Pittman LLP; Holland & Hart LLP; Foley & Lardner LLP; Hilgers Graben PLLC.
Class Definition & Estimated Size
  • Settlement Class: All Persons who purchased or otherwise acquired Ampio common stock between December 29, 2020 and August 2, 2022, inclusive, and were damaged thereby.
  • Exclusions: Defendants; Immediate Family of Individual Defendants; subsidiaries/affiliates of Ampio and its current/former directors and officers; entities in which any Defendant has a controlling interest; legal representatives, agents, heirs, successors-in-interest of excluded parties; and any persons who timely and properly request exclusion.
  • Estimated Class Size: 5,100 (per Sales Bid). Note: ~80% of common shares are held by institutions.
  • No sub-classes are defined in the Settlement Agreement or Preliminary Approval Order.
Gross Settlement Fund & Allocation Plan
  • Gross Settlement Amount: $3,000,000.00 (all-in cash amount, inclusive of all fees, costs, and class member benefits).
  • Settlement Fund = Settlement Amount + interest/accretions earned while in escrow.
  • Deductions from Settlement Fund:
    • Taxes (federal, state, local on income earned by the fund)
    • Notice and Administration Costs (estimated $38,644 per Simpluris bid)
    • Litigation Expenses awarded by the Court
    • Attorneys' fees awarded by the Court
    • Any other Court-approved costs/fees
  • Net Settlement Fund: The balance remaining after deductions, distributed to Authorized Claimants per the Court-approved Plan of Allocation on a pro rata basis based on recognized loss.
  • Escrow Agent: Huntington National Bank.
  • QSF: Settlement Fund is a Qualified Settlement Fund under Treasury Reg. § 1.468B-1.
  • Pre-Effective Date Spending Cap: Up to $75,000 may be advanced from escrow for Notice and Administration Costs and Taxes without further Court order.
  • Number of Distributions: 2 (per Sales Bid).
Method of Notice
  • Postcard Notice (Direct Mail): Mailed by first-class mail to record holders of Ampio common stock, identified via transfer agent data.
  • Broker/Nominee Outreach: Reasonable efforts to reach beneficial owners through broker/nominee forwarding process (nominees must forward within 10 calendar days or provide name lists).
  • Summary Notice (Publication): Published once in Investor's Business Daily and transmitted once over PR Newswire.
  • Settlement Website: Interactive class website (AmpioSecuritiesSettlement.com) hosting the Stipulation, exhibits, Preliminary Approval Order, Notice, and downloadable Claim Form with online claims filing capability.
  • Undeliverable Mail Handling: NCOA search, CASS/DPV verification, skip trace via LexisNexis or similar; remailing of updated addresses.
  • Language: English only.
Method of Disbursement
  • Primary Method: 100% mailed checks (per Sales Bid).
  • 2 distribution cycles anticipated.
  • Estimated Valid Claimants: 1,148 (based on 25% claims rate, deficiency/rejection attrition).

TASK 4 Discrepancy & Risk Analysis
Timeline & Deadline Discrepancies
⚠ Discrepancy #1 — Data Transfer Deadline
  • PAO ¶10: Ampio provides records by October 1, 2024.
  • Stip. ¶20: Within 5 business days of PA entry = October 1, 2024.

Assessment: Consistent. Tight 7-day calendar window — confirm transfer agent readiness.

⚠ Discrepancy #2 — Escrow Funding Timing Ambiguity

Stip. ¶8: $3M due within 20 business days of the later of PA entry or receipt of W-9/wire info. Actual funding date depends on when Lead Counsel delivered banking docs. Action: Confirm W-9 delivery date and actual funding date with counsel.

Scope of Work Discrepancies
⚠ Discrepancy #3 — Email Notice Not in Base Bid

The Stipulation references Notice being "mailed or emailed to Settlement Class Members upon request." The Sales Bid includes only postcard notice and publication; email notice is under Optional Services ($462.40). Action: Clarify if proactive email blast is required or only upon-request fulfillment.

⚠ Discrepancy #4 — Nominee Reimbursement Not Budgeted

PAO ¶12: Reimburse nominees up to $0.70/unit (mailing) or $0.10/name (lists). Sales Bid has no pass-through line item. This is a Settlement Fund cost, not admin fee, but could be significant given ~80% institutional ownership. Action: Monitor against $75K pre-approval spending cap.

⚠ Discrepancy #5 — Final Disposition Letters Not in Base Bid

Final Disposition Letters ($920.60) are Optional Services. If Lead Counsel or the Court requires claimant notification of recognized loss amounts, scope addendum needed.

Class Size & Volume Risks
⚠ Risk #6 — Class Size May Be Understated

The 5,100 estimate likely represents record holders, not beneficial owners. With ~80% institutional ownership, the nominee process could surface significantly more beneficial owners, increasing all volume estimates. Action: Monitor nominee returns; prepare for scope revision.

⚠ Risk #7 — 25% Claims Rate Assumption

Securities class action claims rates vary widely (10–30%). Deviations will impact claims processing, deficiency, and disbursement volumes. Standard estimation risk — monitor closely.

Financial & Administration Risks
✓ No Discrepancy — Settlement Amount

Settlement Agreement (¶tt) specifies $3,000,000.00. Consistent across all documents.

⚠ Risk #8 — Pre-Approval Spending Cap

$75,000 cap vs. $38,644 estimate = comfortable buffer. However, if class size increases via nominee processing, costs could rise. Amounts exceeding $75K pre-Effective Date require Court approval.

✓ No Discrepancy — Language Requirements

English only per Sales Bid. No multilingual requirement in Settlement Agreement or PAO.

Miscellaneous Risks
⚠ Risk #9 — CAFA Notice (Defendants' Obligation)

Not in Simpluris' scope. Monitor that Defendants file CAFA proof by Feb 12, 2025; failure could delay Final Approval.

⚠ Risk #10 — Settlement Termination Triggers

Either party may terminate if opt-outs exceed a confidential threshold (Stip. ¶36). Action: Monitor opt-out volume closely; alert Lead Counsel immediately if approaching threshold.

⚠ Risk #11 — Digital Payments Not Scoped

Sales Bid assumes 100% mailed checks. The Workflow references Wire/ACH as possible methods. If digital payments are requested, scope change required.
